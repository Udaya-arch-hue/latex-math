1983/1. Find all functions $f$ defined on the set of positive real numbers which take positive real values and satisfy the conditions:

$f(xf(y)) = yf(x)$

for all positive real $x,y$;

$f(x)\to 0 \quad \text{as} \quad x\to \infty$

\bigskip

1983/2. Let $A$ be one of the two distinct points of intersection of two unequal coplanar circles $C_1$ and $C_2$ with centers $O_1$ and $O_2$ respectively. One of the common tangents to the circles touches $C_1$ at $P_1$ and $C_2$ at $P_2$, while the other touches $C_1$ at $Q_1$ and $C_2$ at $Q_2$. Let $M_1$ be the midpoint of $P_1Q_1$ and $M_2$ be the midpoint of $P_2Q_2$. Prove that $\angle O_1AO_2=\angle M_1AM_2$.
\vspace{0.2cm}

1983/3. Let $a,b$ and $c$ be pairwise relatively prime positive integers. Prove that $2abc-ab-bc-ca$ is the largest integer which cannot be expressed in the form

$xbc+yca+zab$

where $x,y,z$ are non-negative integers.

\vspace{0.2cm}

1983/4. Let $E$ be a set of points on the sides of an equilateral triangle $ABC$, such that its distance from each of the vertices $A,B,C$ is a rational number. Prove that there exist points $P,Q,R$ in $E$ such that $PQR$ is an equilateral triangle.

\vspace{0.2cm}

1983/5. Is it possible to choose $1983$ distinct positive integers, all not exceeding $10^{5}$, no three of which form an arithmetic progression?
\vspace{0.2cm}

1983/6. Let $a,b,c$ be the lengths of the sides of a triangle. Prove that

$a^2b(a-b)+b^2c(b-c)+c^2a(c-a)\geq0$
